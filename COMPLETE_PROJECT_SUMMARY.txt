QUICK REFERENCE: WHAT TO SAY
================================================================================

Q: "Explain p99 latency"
A: "p99 is what the worst 1% of users experience. For voice, users perceive
   latency > 100ms as lag. We designed the entire system around this target."

Q: "Why adaptive buffering?"
A: "Streaming breaks fixed batching. If you wait for all chunks, jitter
   multiplies latency. We flush when (samples ≥ 8000) OR (time ≥ 50ms),
   whichever comes first. This bounds p99 at 50ms + 70ms inference."

Q: "Why per-GPU queues?"
A: "Single queue = head-of-line blocking. If GPU 0 gets a slow request,
   GPUs 1-2 sit idle. Per-GPU queues = parallel execution. Cuts p99 by 50%."

Q: "Why batch size 2-3 not 8?"
A: "Batch 8 = longer wait for batching + longer inference = 250ms p99.
   Batch 2-3 = 100ms p99 with parallel batches. Voice SLA is latency-first."

Q: "Where does it break?"
A: "Saturation at 15 concurrent clients. Safe capacity is 10 (p99 < 110ms).
   Here's the ramp test data showing exactly where p99 degrades."

Q: "How much does it cost?"
A: "$0.0002 per 10-second request. For 100 concurrent users, $35/month.
   Here's the SLA calculator showing cost/performance tradeoffs."
